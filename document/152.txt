Cyber security is the practice of protecting networks, systems, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information, extorting money from users, or interrupting normal business processes. Cyber security involves the use of a variety of tools, processes, and training to protect networks, systems, and programs from cyber threats. This includes firewalls, malware detection and prevention systems, security protocols, and user training.

Cyber security is a critical component of any organization's IT infrastructure. Cyber threats can come in many forms, such as viruses, malware, phishing attacks, malicious websites, and more. These threats can have serious consequences, including data loss, financial losses, reputation damage, and disruptions to business operations. Cyber security is essential in order to protect an organization's data, systems, and networks from these threats.

Organizations can implement a variety of cyber security measures to protect themselves from cyber threats. These measures include implementing firewalls, using anti-virus software, regularly patching software, training employees on security best practices, and implementing multi-factor authentication. Additionally, organizations should have a clear incident response plan in place in case a cyber attack does occur.
