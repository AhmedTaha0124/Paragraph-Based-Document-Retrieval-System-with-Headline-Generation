Cyber security is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information, extorting money from users, or interrupting normal business processes. Cyber security includes both hardware and software technologies, processes, and practices designed to protect networks, computers, programs, and data from attack, damage, or unauthorized access.

The best way to protect yourself from cyber security threats is to stay informed and be proactive. It is important to install and regularly update anti-virus software, firewalls, and other security measures on all of your devices. Additionally, it is important to be aware of the latest security trends and be cautious when opening emails, clicking on links, or downloading files. Finally, it is important to use strong passwords for all of your accounts and to regularly change them.

Cyber security attacks can be divided into four main categories: malware, phishing, ransomware, and distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks. Malware is malicious software that can be used to access a system, steal data, or delete files. Phishing is the practice of sending emails designed to acquire personal information by pretending to be from a legitimate source. Ransomware is a type of malware that locks computer systems and demands a ransom in exchange for access. Finally, DDoS attacks are attempts to overload a network with large amounts of traffic, making it inaccessible to users.
